Tour Guide Robot (May 2025 - Present)

Study investigates the effects of a mixed-agent team on user satisfaction and learning performance in robot-guided tour scenarios, and to design a new metric, called Following, based on observable human behaviors.

  • Gained extensive hands-on experience with the Toyota Human Support Robot (HSR), operating within the ROS Noetic framework, and demonstrating proficiency with a complex service robot platform.
  • Developed and implemented a robust program for ArUco marker detection compatible with OpenCV 3.0 for the detection and pose estimation of the head
  • Coupled with PID control to precisely center detected markers within the HSR's head camera view, critical for accurate interaction targeting.
  • Developed a specialized program to accurately measure the angle of rotation of the ArUco marker, a key component for objectively quantifying the cognitive following metric when the robot directs participants' attention to specific visual targets.
  • Developed and integrated code for human pose and facial expression estimation utilizing OpenPose and MediaPipe, with a specific focus on extracting facial action units to facilitate nuanced analysis of user emotional responses throughout the tour
  • Conducted user experiments and data analysis
